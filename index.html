Bicycle patterns in Copenhagen

Motivation

In a time where the threat of global warming is ever-present, sustainable modes of transportation are at the forefront of solutions necessary to curb excess emissions.

In Copenhagen, bicycles are an ingrained part of the culture and for many, their favourite mode of transportation. By initially observing the gathered data, there where various questions that came up.

Rising questions

  1. What factors affect the use of bicycles in Copenhagen?
  2. Which are the temporal patterns of the Danes?
  3. How does the weather influence bicycling patterns?
  4. What is impact of Super Bike Lanes and how can the bicyle infrastructure be improved?

Super Bike Lanes

For this we looked at Hareskovvej, as it's the only one from the streets that became a super bike lane during the years we have data for. The rest were delivered at a later time, most of them in 2017.

In the figure on the bottom-right, we plot the "fair comparisons" based on equal number of days on the left side and the possibly biased (in both directions) on the right side. Every point is the average number of cyclists in the specified month. Each point on that same month (vertically) refers to the different years. The dots are colored blue if they are prior to April 2013 and red if they are after it.

We can then observe that more bicyclists passed on Hareskovvej at every month and year after the opening of the super bike lane, as every red point is higher up than the blue ones. This implies that the super bike lanes do have an impact on the number of people who choose to use their bicycle!

Influence of Super Bike Lanes

A pessimistic prediction would be that we see an 23% increase in the number of bicyclists on Hareskovvej, after it was introduced as a super bike lane. Note that this "pessimistic" prediction is much higher than the yearly increase in Copenhagen's population since 2005 (≈2%). At the same time, it is also higher than the general increase of cyclists on the streets since 2005 (≈17%). A more accuracte prediction would probably be to compute the increase of the average number of cyclists before and after April 2013 on Hareskovvej which is: ≈1.60968

This shows that from the data, we can observe that 61% more people on average for the selected four months, ride on Hareskovvej after it's upgrade to "super". This fits very well with other analyses done on the specific location. Newer analyses with data up to 2018, show an increase of 68% on weekdays and 108% during the weekends. Furthermore, it is also worth-mentioning that our work suggest that the government's goal of a 25% increase on the number of cyclists on this super bike lane has been reached!
This was said to save 230 tons of CO2 and 10.1 million DKK, as gain in healthcare economics.
